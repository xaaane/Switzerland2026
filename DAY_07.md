## DAY 7: Interlaken Views & Journey to Zermatt

### September 14th, 2026

**Trip Overview**

A relaxed sightseeing and travel day. You'll spend the morning enjoying the best panoramic view of Interlaken from its local mountain, Harder Kulm. After a leisurely lunch with a view, you will embark on a stunning, world-class train journey that takes you from the Bernese Oberland, through the heart of the Alps, to the iconic car-free village of Zermatt at the foot of the mighty Matterhorn.

---

### **Schedule**

**9:00 AM - 10:00 AM: Leisurely Breakfast & Luggage Logistics**

*   **Action:** Enjoy a relaxed breakfast in Interlaken.
*   **Action:** Check out of your hotel. You have two convenient options for your luggage:
    1.  Leave it with the hotel concierge to retrieve after your morning activity.
    2.  Take it with you and store it in a locker at **Interlaken Ost** train station, your departure point for Zermatt.

**10:00 AM - 1:00 PM: Harder Kulm - Top of Interlaken**

*   **Action:** Make your way to the Harder Kulm funicular station, which is a short walk from Interlaken Ost.
*   **Funicular Ride:** Take the steep, 10-minute funicular ride up the mountainside to Harder Kulm.
*   **Two Lakes Bridge:** At the top, walk out onto the spectacular "Two Lakes Bridge" viewing platform. It offers an incredible panorama of Lake Thun to your right, Lake Brienz to your left, and the iconic peaks of the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau directly ahead.
*   **Relax & Explore:** Spend time soaking in the magnificent views. You can take a short, easy walk around the summit area.
*   **Lunch with a View:** Have an early lunch at the panoramic castle-like restaurant at the top. It's a fantastic spot to eat before your travel afternoon.

**1:00 PM - 1:45 PM: Descend and Prepare for Departure**

*   **Action:** Take the funicular back down to Interlaken.
*   **Action:** Collect your luggage (either from the hotel or the station lockers) and ensure you are at **Interlaken Ost** station, ready for your departure.

**2:00 PM - 4:49 PM: The Scenic Train Journey to Zermatt**

*   **The Journey:** This is one of the great Swiss train journeys. It involves a few simple and efficient changes.
*   **Leg 1 (Interlaken Ost -> Spiez):** Board a train for the short ride to Spiez. This leg offers beautiful views along the shore of Lake Thun.
*   **Leg 2 (Spiez -> Visp):** At Spiez, you will change to a modern train that travels south. A highlight of this leg is passing through the 34.6 km (21.5 mi) **Lötschberg Base Tunnel**, a remarkable feat of engineering that cuts directly through the main Alpine chain.
*   **Leg 3 (Visp -> Zermatt):** In the town of Visp, you will board the narrow-gauge **Matterhorn Gotthard Bahn** train. This is the final, most dramatic hour of the journey. The train climbs steeply up the Matter valley, navigating past charming villages and deep gorges. Keep your eyes looking up the valley, as you will get your first, unforgettable glimpse of the Matterhorn on this leg.

**4:49 PM Onwards: Arrival in Zermatt**

*   **Action:** You will arrive at Zermatt's train station, in the heart of the village.
*   **Car-Free Village:** Welcome to the serene, car-free atmosphere of Zermatt. All local transport is via small electric vehicles (taxis and buses).
*   **Action:** Depending on your hotel's location, you can either enjoy a pleasant walk through the village or take a small electric taxi to check in.
*   **First Impressions:** Spend the early evening taking your first stroll along the main street, Bahnhofstrasse, with the iconic pyramid of the Matterhorn commanding the skyline.

**7:00 PM Onwards: Dinner in Zermatt**

*   **Dining:** Enjoy your first dinner in Zermatt. The village is renowned for its excellent culinary scene, offering everything from cozy restaurants serving traditional cheese fondue and raclette to world-class fine dining.

---

### **Practical Information & Tips**

*   **Train Tickets:** The journey from Interlaken to Zermatt is fully covered by the Swiss Travel Pass. If buying point-to-point tickets, you can purchase the entire journey at once from the SBB website or at the station. Seat reservations are not typically required.
*   **Left vs. Right Side of Train:**
    *   **Spiez to Visp:** It doesn't matter much due to the long tunnel.
    *   **Visp to Zermatt:** Sit on the **left side** of the train (in the direction of travel) for the best views and the first reveal of the Matterhorn.
*   **Zermatt Hotels:** Some hotels offer a complimentary electric shuttle service from the train station. It's worth checking this in advance.
