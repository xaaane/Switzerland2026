## DAY 3: Journey to Wengen & Triple Village Tour

**Trip Overview**

Today you will journey from the Lake Lucerne region deep into the heart of the Bernese Oberland. Your destination is the serene, car-free alpine village of Wengen, perched on a sunny terrace with world-class views. After a scenic trip via the Luzern-Interlaken Express and a series of mountain trains, you will spend the afternoon on a "triple village tour," exploring the magnificent Lauterbrunnen Valley and the cliff-side villages of Mürren and Gimmelwald.

---

### **Schedule**

**Morning (Flexible): Breakfast & Check-out in Lucerne**

*   **Action:** Enjoy a final breakfast in Lucerne.
*   **Action:** Check out of your hotel.

**10:05 AM - 11:55 AM: The Luzern-Interlaken Express**

*   **The Journey:** Board the Luzern-Interlaken Express train to Interlaken Ost.
*   **Tip:** For the best scenery, find a seat on the **right side** of the train in the direction of travel.

**11:55 AM - 12:21 PM: Transfer to Lauterbrunnen**

*   **Action:** At Interlaken Ost station, change to the Berner Oberland-Bahn (BOB) train heading towards **Lauterbrunnen**.

**12:21 PM - 1:00 PM: Ascend to Wengen & Check-in**

*   **Cogwheel Train:** At Lauterbrunnen station, board the green and yellow Wengernalpbahn cogwheel train for the steep 15-minute ascent to Wengen.
*   **Action:** Arrive in Wengen, find your hotel, check in, and drop off your luggage.

**1:00 PM - 2:00 PM: Lunch in Wengen**

*   **Dining:** Enjoy a relaxing lunch at a restaurant in the village, taking in the spectacular views from the terrace.

**2:00 PM - 6:00 PM: Exploring Lauterbrunnen, Mürren & Gimmelwald**

*   **Descend to the Valley:** Take the cogwheel train back down to **Lauterbrunnen** and walk to the iconic **Staubbach Falls**.
*   **Journey to Mürren:**
    *   From Lauterbrunnen, go to the **Grütschalp cable car** station (Lauterbrunnen BLM).
    *   Ride the cable car up to **Grütschalp**, then take the historic narrow-gauge train along the cliff edge to **Mürren**.
*   **Explore Mürren:** Walk through the beautiful village of Mürren, heading from the train station towards the Schilthornbahn cable car station at the other end.
*   **Descend to Gimmelwald:**
    *   **Hike (Recommended):** Take the easy, well-signed, and paved downhill path to Gimmelwald. It's a beautiful 25-30 minute walk.
    *   **Alternative:** Or, take the Schilthornbahn cable car one stop down.
*   **Explore Gimmelwald:** Spend a few moments exploring this tiny, authentic hamlet. It's a true step back in time.

**6:00 PM Onwards: Scenic "Valley Loop" Return to Wengen**

*   **Cable Car to the Valley Floor:** From Gimmelwald, take the Schilthornbahn cable car the rest of the way down to **Stechelberg**.
*   **PostBus Journey:** At Stechelberg, board the PostBus for a scenic drive along the valley floor back to Lauterbrunnen, passing numerous waterfalls.
*   **Final Ascent:** At Lauterbrunnen station, take the familiar cogwheel train for the final ascent back up to Wengen.

**7:30 PM Onwards: Dinner in Wengen**

*   **Dining:** After a full day of travel and exploration, enjoy a well-deserved dinner in the peaceful, magical atmosphere of Wengen at night.

---

### **Practical Information & Tips**

*   **Swiss Travel Pass:** Your pass covers this entire, incredible loop for free: Lucerne to Wengen, the Grütschalp route to Mürren, the Schilthornbahn from Mürren down to Stechelberg, and the PostBus to Lauterbrunnen.
*   **Luggage Transfer:** Consider using the SBB luggage service to send your main bags from Lucerne to Wengen to make your travel day easier.
*   **Car-Free Serenity:** Embrace the quiet! Wengen, Mürren, and Gimmelwald are all car-free. All transport is by train, cable car, or on foot, which is a huge part of their charm.
*   **Check Timetables:** It's always wise to have a general idea of the last connections for your return journey, but service is generally frequent until early evening.
*   **The Mürren-Gimmelwald Walk:** This short walk is highly recommended over taking the cable car between the two villages as it allows you to truly appreciate the landscape at a slower pace.
